Generated by `tools/validation/rockphysics/genfixtures.py` from
`oracle.py` — independent stdlib-Python reference implementations
(dual-implementation rule: the G6.1 JS engines validate against these

## Provenance

Every numeric constant in the oracle was cross-checked on 2026-07-13
against open, tested implementations rather than written from memory:

- **Batzle & Wang 1992** brine/water: bruges (Agile Scientific).
  This check caught a real error — the brine-velocity S² coefficient
  is **−820**, not the −1820 sometimes misquoted.
- **Batzle & Wang 1992** gas Z-factor/K and dead/live oil:
  equinor/open_petro_elastic (`material/batzle_wang/`). Live-oil
  density follows that implementation: eq 24 as written (B₀ carries
  the temperature dependence, no extra eq-18/19 correction); live-oil
  velocity is eq 20a on the eq-22 pseudo-density.
- **Greenberg–Castagna 1992** coefficients: rockphypy (sand/shale)
  and auralib (limestone/dolomite, mudrock line).
- **Exact Zoeppritz PP**: the Dvorkin et al. (2014) expression form
  as published in bruges `reflection.py`; anchored to the analytic
  normal-incidence identity, the small-contrast Aki–Richards limit,
  and the vanishing-Vs acoustic (fluid–fluid) coefficient.
- **Tuning**: the Ricker equal-and-opposite-pair tuning thickness is
  the peak-to-trough time √6/(2πf) — Kallweit & Wood (1982)
  approximate it as 1/(2.6·f_dom); the golden (16 ms at 25 Hz, 1 ms
  grid) matches to within one sample.

## Conventions

- SI in the goldens: densities kg/m³, velocities m/s, moduli Pa.
  (Batzle-Wang functions run internally in the paper's native units:
  °C, MPa, g/cc, weight-fraction NaCl, GOR in L/L.)
- AVO curves store exact Zoeppritz (real and imaginary parts),
  Aki–Richards, and 2-/3-term Shuey per angle 0–40°, plus Shuey A/B/C
  and the Rutherford–Williams class (|A| ≤ 0.02 band for class II —
  documented convention; the literature draws that band
  qualitatively).
- The four AVO interface fixtures are constructed shale-over-sand
  cases, self-asserted to classify I/II/III/IV.
